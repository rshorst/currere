Regressive — returning to the past

Return to your educational past and observe it — not to relive it uncritically, but to examine what sedimented in you through schooling, family, community, and place. What taught you before school named it? What have you been carrying without knowing?

"The regressive involves description and analysis of one's intellectual biography or, if you prefer, educational past." — Pinar, 1975

Progressive — imagining the future

Describe an imagined future — not a plan, but a vision. What might you become? What futures does your educational history open, or foreclose? The progressive holds the horizon against which the past can be read differently.

"The progressive involves a description of one's imagined future." — Pinar, 1975

Analytical — holding past and future together

Hold past and future in tension and look for pattern and contradiction. What do you see in yourself when past and future are examined together? What silences appear? This is not a clinical analysis but a disciplined act of self-attention.

"The analytical calls for a psychoanalysis of one's phenomenologically described educational present, past, and future." — Pinar, 1975

Synthetical — returning to the present, changed

Return to the present with new understanding. The synthetical places your integrated self-knowledge into the larger political and cultural web — tracing the dialectical relation between your individual biography and the social structures that shaped it.

"The synthetic totalizes the fragments of educational experience and places this integrated understanding into the larger political and cultural web." — Pinar, 1975
