# currere
Interactive visualization of Pinar's (1975) Currere method

An interactive diagram of William Pinar's method of currere, developed for use on the ETEC 531 Canvas course page.
The four moments — regressive, progressive, analytical, and synthetical — are drawn as overlapping rings to reflect Pinar's insistence that currere is not a linear sequence but a recursive practice. You may enter at any moment and move in any direction.
The content of each panel draws directly from Pinar's original formulation in Curriculum Theorizing: The Reconceptualists (1975) and Toward a Poor Curriculum, co-authored with Madeleine Grumet (1976), as well as from course notes developed by Stephen Petrina for the Department of Curriculum and Pedagogy at UBC (2014).
